The Game

What is Shadowdark?

Shadowdark is a tabletop roleplaying game of fantasy adventure — part dungeon crawl, part survival, entirely unlike anything you have played before. It strips the genre down to its bones: you are fragile, the dark is real, and the dungeon does not care whether you live.

There are no safe rooms. There are no quest markers. There is no map already drawn for you. There is only a torch burning down, a dungeon full of things that want you dead, and the decisions your character makes in the next ten seconds.

It is fast, brutal, and utterly thrilling — a game where escaping with your life feels like a triumph, and a first-level adventurer with a torch and a sword can become a legend or a footnote.

I
Darkness Kills

Torches burn for one real hour. When the last one goes out, the dark becomes a predator. Light is not a convenience — it is life itself.

II
Death is Real

Characters die. Sometimes quickly, sometimes gloriously. This is not failure — it is the game. Every session carries genuine stakes because the reaper is always nearby.

III
Choices Matter

There are no guaranteed outcomes. Every door opened, every fight taken, every torch lit is a calculated risk. The dungeon rewards clever thinking over brute force.

IV
The World Breathes

Factions scheme, monsters patrol, alliances crumble. The dungeon is not a static backdrop — it is a living ecosystem that reacts to everything your party does.

Setting

The World You Will Enter

This is not a world of gleaming heroes and noble quests. It is a world of crumbling empires and forgotten gods, where civilisations have risen and been swallowed by the dark more times than anyone can remember. The wilderness is genuine wilderness. The ruins are genuinely ruinous.

🌑
The Dark

Darkness is absolute. Creatures born in it see perfectly. You do not. Every dungeon is their home and your nightmare.

⚔️
Ancient Violence

Warrior-cults, blood rituals, gods of rage and ruin — the old civilisations did not fall gently. Their legacies are dangerous and magnificent.

💀
The Cost of Power

Magic and glory come with prices. The greatest treasures are guarded by the worst things imaginable. Nothing valuable is left undefended.

🔥
Survival First

Getting out alive is the first victory. Getting out rich is the second. Getting out having changed the world? That is the stuff of legend.

The Adventure

The Lost Citadel

A Dungeon That Tells a Story

Somewhere in the arid scrubland, half-consumed by sand, a citadel of coral-red columns and vaulted dark chambers still stands. Its gates are open. Its halls have not been silent for centuries. Something inside is still very much alive.

Long ago, a warrior-cult dwelt within these walls — fierce, bloodthirsty, devoted to the bull. Their rituals grew darker with each generation until, one night, something catastrophic happened within. In a single night of slaughter, everything they had built was reduced to corpses and shadows.

The citadel was never reclaimed. Whatever horror was unleashed never left. And now, for reasons of your own, you stand before its doors.

Something Still Walks These Halls

The citadel is not empty. More than one force has staked a claim to the darkness inside — and none of them will welcome you. Some will try to use you before they try to kill you. Others will skip straight to the killing. You will learn which is which the hard way.

What People Say

Rumours from the Road

Before you reached the citadel, you heard things. In taverns and market towns, from other travellers, drunks and desperate scholars.

  • "Ancient kings who dwelt in the citadel possessed magical weapons that were feared far and wide."
  • "A savage beast drenched in blood stalks the citadel's halls."
  • "Piles of gems and gold lie about as if totally forgotten."
  • "Beware when touching the body of a dead citadel dweller."
  • "Opportunistic, grey-furred beastmen haunt the shadows."
  • "The citadel is rife with secret rooms and passageways."

Some of this is true  ·  Some of this is legend  ·  Some of this is bait

Your Story

Why You Are Here

You did not arrive at this citadel by accident. Someone brought you here — someone with coin, a story, and reasons they have not fully shared. They want something from inside. They are paying you to get it.

Whether that arrangement holds, whether the reasons turn out to be what you were told, and whether whoever sent you here can be trusted — these are questions the dungeon may answer for you before your employer does.

For now: the doors are ahead of you, carved with bucking bulls mid-leap. Your torch is lit. The dark waits inside.

Character Creation

Who Are You?

Before the session you will each build a character — your voice, your blade, your candle in the dark. Two choices define the core of who that character is: their Ancestry and their Class.

Ancestries

A Note on Ancestry

This campaign is rooted in a human world — its cities, its politics, its ruins, its wars were built and broken by human hands. Playing a human means you are the default of this setting, not the exception. You move through it without the sideways glances a goblin draws, without the suspicion an elf carries. You belong here, and that opens doors that nothing else does.

Mechanically, humans begin with a powerful advantage: they gain an extra Talent roll at first level, meaning your human character is more developed and more distinctly themselves from the very first session. Other ancestries are available and valid — all have their strengths and shortcomings.

★ Recommended
Human
Bold  ·  Adaptable  ·  Diverse

The most common people in the world — merchants, soldiers, priests, thieves, wanderers. They built the citadel you are about to enter. They also destroyed it.

Ambitious: Gain one additional talent. No other ancestry does this. It makes a meaningful difference from the very start.

Available
Half-Orc
Towering  ·  Tusked  ·  Relentless

Born between two worlds and fully welcome in neither. Half-orcs carry the strength of orcish blood alongside a human upbringing — or a hard road instead of one.

Mighty: Gain a bonus to attack and damage with melee weapons. Pure violence.

Available
Elf
Ethereal  ·  Graceful  ·  Ancient

Long-lived and alien in subtle ways — elves remember things that humans have forgotten and carry a perspective shaped by centuries rather than decades.

Farsight: Gain a bonus to ranged attacks or spellcasting checks. Precise where others are forceful.

Available
Dwarf
Brave  ·  Stalwart  ·  Sturdy

Dwarves come from the stone and return to it. They are slower to move and slower to break than anyone else in the party. In a dungeon, that is worth a great deal.

Stout: Start with additional HP and gain it faster than others. Their bodies resist punishment that would floor others.

Available
Goblin
Green  ·  Clever  ·  Thrive in Dark

Small, fast, and treated as a joke until the moment they are not. Goblins thrive in cramped spaces and chaotic situations — exactly what a dungeon provides.

Keen Senses: You can't be surprised. When everything is suddenly on fire... you probably lit the match.

Available
Halfling
Small  ·  Cheerful  ·  Mischievous

Halflings are easy to dismiss and hard to kill. Their size is their advantage — they have a way of surviving situations that should have ended them.

Stealthy: Once per day, you can become invisible for 3 rounds. Unseen even to those who see in the dark.

Classes

Your class defines what you actually do when the doors open and the dark rushes in. Each one fills a different role — and a party that covers all four is a party that survives.

⚔️
Fighter

The one who stands in front. The reason everyone else gets to run when things go wrong.

Fighters hit hardest, take the most punishment, and keep moving when others fall. Weapon Mastery lets them specialise — a fighter with a greataxe is a different instrument of violence than one with twin blades. Start here if you want to solve problems directly.

🗡️
Thief

The one who finds the things no one else was supposed to find — and takes them.

Thieves pick locks, find traps before they trigger, and deal devastating Backstab damage from the shadows. In a dungeon full of secrets, a thief is the party's most valuable asset — if they survive long enough to use their skills.

✝️
Priest

The one who speaks to powers older than the citadel — and occasionally those powers answer.

Priests channel divine magic to heal, protect, and occasionally smite. Turn Undead makes them essential the moment bones start walking — and the dungeon is full of dead things that refuse to stay that way.

📖
Wizard

The one carrying power they barely understand, contained in words that must never be mispronounced.

Wizards cast from a spellbook. Their magic is devastating and unpredictable — one spell can change everything, but spells run out and wizards are fragile. Intelligence governs everything. They die easily and matter enormously.

A Taste of What You Might Carry

Weapon
Greatsword

Two hands. 1d12 damage. The statement that you intend to end the conversation.

Weapon
Dagger

Fast and precise. Open them up and finish the argument with finesse.

Weapon
Longbow

1d8 from a safe distance. The wisdom of never being in reach of whatever you're killing.

Weapon
Staff

The wizard's last resort. Less elegant than a spell. Considerably more reliable when the spells are spent.

Fighter Talent
Hauler

You carry more than anyone else. In a game where every slot matters, this is more powerful than it sounds.

Wizard Spell
Sleep

A group of enemies folds to the floor, unconscious. The most diplomatic spell in the book.

Priest Spell

Lay hands on a dying ally and give them back to the living. One of the most important things in a dungeon.

Thief Talent
Backstab

Strike from hiding for double damage. Set it up correctly and one hit ends a fight.

Before You Play

What Kind of Game This Is

Shadowdark is an old-school game with modern bones. A few things are worth knowing before you sit down at the table so none of them catch you off guard.

Running Away is Heroic

Some things in this dungeon cannot be fought — not at first level, not head-on. Knowing when to flee is not cowardice. It is survival, and survival is the first skill of any great adventurer.

Death Happens

Characters can and will die. When they do, a new character can enter play within minutes. This is not a failure state — it is a story beat. Every death makes the dungeon feel more real.

Explore Everything

The dungeon rewards curiosity. Inscriptions, mosaics, old bones — every detail is there for a reason. The players who look at everything survive longer than those who only look at monsters.

Talk Before You Fight

Not everything in the citadel wants you dead immediately. Some things are afraid. Some things are greedy. Some can be reasoned with — or at least pointed at something else.

Preparation

What You Need to Play

Shadowdark is deliberately lean. Everything you need will be provided — just bring yourself and a willingness to make questionable decisions in dark places.

📜
The Rules

Your GM will provide everything you need. Read what you are given — it is short, and it will save your life.

⚔️
A Character

Made in minutes. Choose your ancestry, choose your class, roll your stats, take your gear. Arrive knowing who you are.

🕯️
Your Imagination

No miniatures, no battle maps — just descriptions, decisions, and dice. The dungeon lives in the mind's eye.

Tips Before Your First Session

  • Light is your most important resource. Bring as much of it as your inventory can carry. You will not regret having too much, and you will absolutely regret having too little.
  • Talk to your party before the session. Who are these people to each other? Why do they trust one another enough to walk into a place like this together? A sentence each is enough.
  • Every inventory slot is a decision. You cannot carry everything. What you bring reflects what you think you'll face. Think carefully — and bring rope.
  • Describe what you do, not what you roll. Instead of "I search the room," say "I check inside the stone basin and run my hand along the base." The more specific you are, the more the dungeon gives back.
  • Cover each other's weaknesses. A party of four fighters will die quickly and expensively. A party with one of each class can survive almost anything — including each other.
  • Make peace with loss before you start. Characters die in Shadowdark. Gear is lost. Plans fall apart. The players who embrace this have far more fun than the ones who fight it.
